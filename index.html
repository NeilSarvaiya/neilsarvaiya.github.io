Avid Reader

As the title says, I like to think of myself as an avid reader. It started as a recommendation from my English teacher to help with reading comprehension because, in her words, my vocabulary was garbage (politely). I had an 85 by the way. The first book I read was Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (yes, I started with the third one). Honestly, I barely remember it. Back then, finishing a book felt more satisfying than actually learning from it.

Now, I'd say my reading maturity has improved. I actually enjoy the books I read, and finishing the last page usually leaves me feeling the saddest, at least most of the time. I also like to do tiny book reviews on my X account.

Badminton

Oh badminton, my beloved. You made me work for you, but you were my first real obsession. For anyone wondering, badminton isn't a girl, it's a sport. It all started during covid when I was a big chunky kid and my parents asked my brother to take me to play so I could lose weight. From the moment I picked up a racket, I was hooked. From Grade 6 to Grade 9, I'd go to the rec centre every day after school, join a club, and spend hours watching videos on footwork and drills. My goal was to become the Edmonton City Champion, and eventually, I was one.

The picture on the right with me and my brother was right after I won city championships. My brother had coached me through the whole process and he himself is actually a city champion in a city called Kamloops, sharing this moment was really special to me. (I'm taller then him now don't worry). Even though I don't play as much now, badminton taught me focus, discipline, and consistency. It got me into fitness and dieting and showed me how to learn from people with more experience.

Making Videos

The iterative conceptualization and synthesis of digital communicative assets for targeted consumption, or in other words, content creation, is something I've done as a hobby back when I was 7 years old. Back then, I was making amateur skits and gaming videos, and it was only recently that I actually made the idea of Naturally Curious. I always liked the idea of making something and having it be open for millions of people to see. Even if there aren't millions of people actually watching it, I think it's cool to know that it's possible to make a community of people who think exactly like you.

Honestly though, when I first told the people around me about my videos, I unfortunately was met with a lot of criticism and backlash for trying to make something as ambitious as a successful Youtube Channel, but at the same time, I believe it's important to try things that you genuinely enjoy even if some people find it out of the ordinary.
